What Green Building Covers.

Pakistan energy crisis makes green building not just an environmental choice but a financial one. Buildings with solar systems, efficient insulation, and optimized energy design significantly outperform conventional buildings on monthly operating costs and increasingly on rental appeal and resale value.

Bitoreal Green Building provides the full range of sustainable building services including solar system design and installation, energy audits, building efficiency planning, and green certification advisory.

We approach sustainability practically with solutions that are available in Pakistan, financeable, and maintainable by the local market.

What’s Included
  • Solar energy system design and installation for rooftop and ground-mounted systems
  • Energy audit covering current consumption analysis and efficiency opportunity identification
  • Building energy efficiency design covering insulation, glazing, shading, and ventilation
  • Green building certification advisory for LEED, EDGE, and Pakistan green standards
  • Rainwater harvesting system design and installation
  • Sustainable material specification for new builds and retrofits
What’s Not Included
  • Large-scale commercial solar farms as we focus on residential and commercial building scale
  • Grid infrastructure work beyond the building connection point
  • NEPRA licensing which the client applies for and we coordinate

Real Client Scenarios.

How Bitoreal has helped Pakistani clients with green building.

Residential Solar Installation in Islamabad

A family in F-7 Islamabad with monthly electricity bills of PKR 45,000 installed a 10kW rooftop solar system. After installation their bill dropped to under PKR 8,000 per month, a saving of PKR 37,000 monthly. The system paid back within 4.2 years at current electricity rates.

Green Building Design for New Construction

A developer building a 12-unit apartment complex engaged us at design stage to integrate green building features. We specified enhanced insulation, optimized window placement, a solar system sized for common area loads, and a rainwater harvesting system. The resulting building achieved 35 percent lower energy consumption than a conventional equivalent.
